I am Ji Guan, an [Associate Professor](https://people.ucas.ac.cn/~guanji) at the Institute of Software, Chinese Academy of Sciences. I received my bachelor's degree in computational mathematics from [Sichuan University](http://en.scu.edu.cn), and my Ph.D. in quantum computing from the [Centre for Quantum Software and Information](https://www.uts.edu.au/research-and-teaching/our-research/centre-quantum-software-and-information), [University of Technology Sydney](https://www.uts.edu.au), supervised by [Mingsheng Ying](https://www.uts.edu.au/staff/mingsheng.ying) and [Yuan Feng](https://www.cs.tsinghua.edu.cn/csen/info/1312/4483.htm).

I lead the FM4QC group, working on formal methods for quantum computing. My research focuses on the security, privacy, correctness, and reliability of quantum algorithms, programs, and hardware systems. I am particularly interested in model checking of quantum systems, trustworthy quantum machine learning, quantum privacy, and quantum-enhanced verification algorithms.

For more details, see [Research](./research), [Publications](./publications), [Recruitment](./recruitment), and [CV](./cv).

## Research Interests

- Formal verification and model checking of quantum systems
- Robustness, privacy, and safety of quantum machine learning
- Logical and automata-theoretic methods for quantum programs
- Efficient analysis, simulation, and optimization of noisy quantum circuits
- Quantum algorithms for verification and certification tasks
